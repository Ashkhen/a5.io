I think of good design as a marriage between analytical and creative thinking.
I'm passionate about the entire product design process starting with an early concept
and culminating in the delivery of products that are awesome to interact with.

I always wanted to create awesome products! I taught myself to code in Ruby, Python, and Javascript, soon to realize that I enjoy all phases of the product development lifecycle, so I decided to focus on bridging the gap between Business, Engineering and Design.

In a past life, I was the Director of Marketing at an Autodesk VAR, a co-founder at a small clothing design and alteration shop, and a freelance marketing consultant.

I hold a B.S. degree in Global Management from UC Berkeley,
Haas School of Business.

Research

In the research phase of the design process, I conduct stakeholder and user interviews to scope out the project. I then perform competitive analysis, conduct online surveys and contextual inquiries in order to develop use cases and personas.

Concept Development

To develop concepts, I start out by sketching initial ideas. I card sort to help finalize the information architecture of the design, then create site maps and user flows. Wireframes are then created to be used in prototyping.

Testing

I create prototypes to test the usability of the design. I use heuristic evaluation principles to determine the severity of flaws that come up during usability testing. I incorporate user feedback to create updated iterations of the prototype.
